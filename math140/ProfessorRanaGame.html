Game: Integrating Your Differential Decisions

The Calculus Collaboration Challenge: Your team's **"Differential Decisions"** will be integrated into your final score!

1. Choose Your Roles (Teams of 3-4)

Before the first problem, each group must assign the following three mandatory roles:

  • The Leader:

    Facilitates discussion. After the timer goes off, the leader asks a new person to share their progress, for instance, ‚ÄúAlex, can you share your thoughts and progress first?‚Äù

  • The Writer:

    Records the final, group-agreed answer on a notecard to be submitted.

  • The Differentiator:

    Decides the group's **Instantaneous Rate of Change** (i.e., the derivative) by choosing how many points to risk: **1, 2, or 3 points** (1 = least confident, 3 = most confident). This "differential decision" determines how much your score changes each round.

2. The Problem Solving Cycle

  1. Teacher Presents: The teacher describes a calculus problem on the board and sets a timer.
  2. Individual Work: Everyone works quietly and individually on the problem while the timer runs.
  3. Discussion Phase: When the timer goes off, the Leader of each group begins the discussion to synthesize solutions.
  4. Submission:
    • The Writer records the final answer on a notecard.
    • The Differentiator decides on the risk points (1, 2, or 3) and records them.

3. Special Item: The Magic Star

Occasionally, the teacher will pass out the **Magic Star** to one lucky team.

If you possess the Magic Star, your Differentiator can decide (before you see the next problem) to risk a much larger range of points: 1, 2, 3, ..., up to 10 points!

Use this power wisely‚Äîit can help teams catch up quickly!

4. Review and Results

  • Immediate Feedback: After all groups submit their answers, the teacher will go over the solution on the board, slowly and clearly.
  • Points Awarded/Lost (Integration):

    Your **Differentiator's** choice of points is the **Instantaneous Rate of Change** for your score. If your group's answer is correct, you EARN those points (positive change). If your answer is incorrect, you LOSE those points (negative change).

  • BONUS RULE (Teacher Mistake): If the teacher makes a mistake and gets the answer wrong:
    • Teams with Correct Answers: Double the points they risked! (e.g., risk 3, earn 6).
    • Teams with Incorrect Answers: Do NOT lose any points. (e.g., risk 3, lose 0).
Good luck, and may the derivatives be ever in your favor!
