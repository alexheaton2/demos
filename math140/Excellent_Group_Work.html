T-Chart for Excellent Group Work

This chart outlines the observable behaviors of a highly effective learning group. Use it as a guide for your own participation and to help your group stay on track.

Component 1: Full Engagement

Everyone is focused, on-task, and mentally present.

Looks Like... Sounds Like... Avoid...
Leaning in toward the group "So, what's our first step?" Leaning back, arms crossed
Eyes on the shared work or on the speaker "Let's try this approach." Staring out the window or at the clock
Nodding, taking notes "I see what you did there." Having a phone visible
Actively writing, calculating, or manipulating models "Are we all ready to move on?" Doodling, packing up early

Component 2: Rich Mathematical Discourse

Members use precise language to explain their thinking and deepen understanding.

Looks Like... Sounds Like... Avoid...
Pointing to specific parts of an equation or graph "I used the chain rule because this is a composite function." Vague language ("that thingy," "you know...")
Drawing diagrams to explain a concept "Can you explain why you took the derivative of that part?" Stating answers without justification
Referencing notes or Khan Academy to support a claim "The limit doesn't exist since the left and right sides don't agree." Unexplained guessing
Writing out steps clearly for everyone to see "Let's define our variables first." One person quietly working ahead

Component 3: Supportive Teamwork & Shared Responsibility

The group functions as a team where every member's voice is valued and all members are accountable for the group's progress.

Looks Like... Sounds Like... Avoid...
Turning to a quieter member to ask their opinion "What do you think about this step?" Interrupting or talking over someone
Taking turns writing or leading the discussion "I'll write this part, can you lead the next one?" One person doing all the writing and talking
Giving a thumbs-up or encouraging nod "That's a great question, let's figure it out." Dismissing questions ("That's easy.")
All members looking at the shared work, not just their own "Does everyone understand how we got that? Do we all agree?" One person dominating or making all decisions
