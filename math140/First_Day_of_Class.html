First Day of Class

Part 1: The Hook

Why Calculus is Amazing

Application 1: Predicting Earthquakes

Calculus helps model vibrations and analyze seismic data to understand—and one day predict—earthquakes.

Application 2: The Nature of Black Holes

To describe how spacetime warps near a black hole, physicists use integrals—a core concept of calculus.

Instructor Note: Play for ~2 minutes.

Application 3: Mathematical Microscopes

Derivatives and integrals act like "mathematical microscopes" to zoom in on a single point or zoom out to see the big picture.

Instructor Note: Play the first ~2 minutes.

Application 4: How Things Change

The rules that govern how things change, smooth out, and diffuse are called differential equations—the language of calculus.

Application 5: The Limits of Financial Markets

The idea of a "perfectly efficient market" is a theoretical ideal. In calculus, we call this a limit—the very first topic of this course.

Instructor Note: Watch from 1:00-1:36, then skip to 6:41.
