Activity Guide: Unit Test Takedown

Your Mission

Work together in your small group to take an official Khan Academy Unit Test. Your goal is to collectively achieve a **100% score**. This is a great way to prepare for your upcoming exam.

This activity is all about collaborative problem-solving and peer tutoring. You are encouraged to use any resource on the Khan Academy site—videos, articles, or hints from Khanmigo—to work through problems you don't understand. Do not use resources outside of Khan Academy (no ChatGPT or WolframAlpha or anything else).

Before You Start: Please read all of the directions in the "How to Play" section below before beginning the unit test.

Important: This unit test is a review activity and is **not for a grade**. The goal is to learn together, not to perform under pressure.

How to Play: The Rules of Engagement

  1. Get Started

    As a group, open up a Khan Academy Unit Test on one person's device. For example, you can find the test on the page for Unit 2: Differentiation, or another unit your instructor assigns.

  2. Assign Roles

    Decide on who will perform each of these three roles. Everyone should help out, but these people have special responsibilities:

    Reader/Leader: Reads the problem and instructions out loud. After the silent timer is up, this person calls on each group member to share their answer and thinking.

    Timer: Responsible for setting a 2-minute timer and making sure nobody uses resources outside of Khan Academy.

    Submitter: Manages the device with the unit test and is in charge of entering the group's final, agreed-upon answer.

  3. The 2-Minute Rule: Think First!

    When a new problem appears, the Timer starts a **2-minute timer**. For these two minutes, everyone works **silently and individually**. Do not talk or use any resources. This is your chance to try the problem on your own first.

  4. Collaborate and Conquer

    After the 2-minute timer goes off, the Reader/Leader asks everyone to share their approach. Discuss your answers. If you disagree, defend your reasoning. If someone is stuck, work together to explain the concept. Once your group agrees on a final answer, the Submitter enters it into the test.

  5. Use Your Resources

    If the entire group is stuck on a problem, that's a great sign that you've found a concept to review! Use the resources on Khan Academy—watch a video, read an article, or ask Khanmigo for a hint—to learn the material together before attempting the problem again.

Keys to Great Teamwork

Everyone Participates, No One Dominates

Make sure every voice is heard. If you've been talking a lot, invite someone else to share. If you've been quiet, it's your job to contribute your thoughts.

Explain Your Thinking

Don't just give an answer. The most important part is explaining *how* you got it. The goal is for everyone to understand the process, not just the result.

Disagree with Ideas, Not People

Healthy debate is good! It’s okay to challenge a teammate's approach, but always do it respectfully. Say "Can you explain that a different way?" instead of "You're wrong."

Encourage Each Other

A little encouragement goes a long way. Use phrases like "Good idea," "Let's try that," and "Thanks for explaining." A positive team environment helps everyone learn.

Goal and Mindset

  • Focus on the Process, Not Just the Answer. The most valuable part of this activity is the discussion. Explaining a concept to a teammate is one of the best ways to solidify your own understanding.
  • Identify Your Weak Spots. The goal isn't just to get 100%, but to figure out *why* you might miss a problem. Pay attention to the questions that cause the most debate in your group—these are excellent topics to review more before the exam.
  • Be a Good Teammate. Listen to everyone's ideas and be patient. The goal is for everyone in the group to leave feeling more confident than when they started.
