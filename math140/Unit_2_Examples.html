Secants & Average Rate of Change

The average rate of change is the slope of the secant line between two points on a curve. It tells us the function's average "speed" over an interval.

Metaphor: Think of a road trip. Your average speed is the total distance divided by the total time. This doesn't tell you your speed at any specific moment, but it gives you the overall rate of change for the entire trip.

Graphs of Functions & Their Derivatives

The derivative graph, f'(x), tells you the slope of the original function, f(x), at every point. Where f(x) goes up, f'(x) is positive. Where f(x) has a peak or valley, f'(x) is zero.

Metaphor: Imagine hiking on a mountain, f(x). The derivative, f'(x), is a device that tells you the steepness of the trail. It shows a positive number going uphill, zero at the peak, and a negative number going downhill.

Derivative as Slope of Tangent Line

The derivative at a single point gives us the instantaneous rate of change. This is the slope of the tangent line—the line that just "touches" the curve at that one point.

Metaphor: Back to the road trip. The instantaneous rate of change is what your speedometer reads at a specific moment. It's the "slope" of your journey at that exact instant.

The Limit Definition of the Derivative

This is the mathematical machine we use to find the exact slope of the tangent line. We find the slope of a secant line over an interval 'h', then shrink 'h' to zero.

Metaphor: Think of a high-resolution camera zooming in on a curve. As you zoom in infinitely close (letting h → 0), any small piece of the curve starts to look like a straight line. The limit definition is the lens of our camera.

Estimating Derivatives from Tables

When we only have a table of values, we can't find the exact derivative. Instead, we get a good estimate by calculating the average rate of change between the two closest points.

Metaphor: Imagine you only have photos of a car's location at 1:59 PM and 2:01 PM. You can't know its exact speed at 2:00, but you can calculate its average speed between those two times to get the best estimate.
