Application 1: Differential Equations

  • Solves systems by decoupling them into eigenvectors.
  • Nonlinear systems are linearized near equilibria (the most important solutions).

Application 2: Fourier Decomposition

Application 3: Physics & The Wave Equation

  • Because the wave equation is linear, white light is a linear combination of pure colors.
  • We can use linear algebra and commuting operators in infinite-dimensional function spaces.
  • Solutions are eigenfunctions of certain operators, or the kernel or nullspace of an operator.

Application 5: General Relativity

  • Parallel Transport is a linear operator between tangent spaces.
  • Curvature (Gravity) is described by a Tensor (family of linear maps).
  • Linear algebra is crucial even in nonlinear theories like Einstein's GR.
